About

I enjoy using Linux (Fedora Workstation with GNOME is my distro of choice). If you don't know what Linux and FOSS (for these are my passions) are, then firstly; what are you doing with your life? and secondly, I'm sad that the closed-source world and ecosystems are taking over that much. 'Linux', first of all, is a family of Unix-like, open-source computer operating systems. It's based off the Linux Kernel, an operating system kernel by Linus Torvalds, first released on September 17, 1991. Linux is typically packaged in a Linux Distribituion, or 'distro'. This open source aspect of Linux as a whole is one of my favorite aspects of it. Along with the customizability, stability, community, and control of your hardware. With a Linux operating system you can truly do with your hardware as it was meant: to compute as you wish, with freedom and elegance
FOSS, is an acronym for 'Free Open Source Software'. And it is nearly as wonderful as Linux. 'Free', of course means 'Libre', or 'unrestrained' in this instance, though FOSS is often free-of-charge.
